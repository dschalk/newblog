Reliability Champions
The second generation Honda CRV (2002-2007) features a 2.4L K24 engine that _routinely exceeds 300,000_ miles without major repairs, outperforming newer crossovers in longevity.

Toyota's 4Runner (1996-2002) remains a benchmark for reliability, offering fully boxed frames and durable interiors for $3,000-$5,000 with under 200,000 miles.

Toyota Land Cruiser 80 and early 100 series (1996-2002) are engineered to conquer harsh terrain and extreme winters, with bulletproof mechanicals and intelligent 4WD systems.

Hidden Gems
The 1999-2004 Jeep Grand Cherokee WJ with its 4.0L inline-6 engine easily surpasses 200,000 miles, representing a sweet spot before aggressive cost-cutting measures.

First-generation Toyota Highlander (2001-2007) transformed the Camry platform into a durable SUV, with its 3.0L V6 engine rarely needing internal repairs even after 250,000 miles.

Early Lexus RX models (1998-2005) offer luxurious reliability under $5,000, with 3.0L and 3.3L V6 engines known to exceed 300,000 miles when properly maintained.

Surprising Longevity
The 2001-2007 Ford Escape with 2.3L Duratec engine is unexpectedly overbuilt, capable of 250,000+ miles with basic maintenance and featuring a trouble-free automatic transmission.

2000-2006 Chevrolet Tahoe/GMC Yukon (GMT 800 platform) boasts the legendary 5.3L Vortec V8, an overbuilt engine with deep skirt iron block and generous tolerances, routinely achieving 300,000+ miles.
