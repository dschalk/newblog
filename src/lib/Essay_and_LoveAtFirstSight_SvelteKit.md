## Love as Instinct: A Natural Account of a Human Experience

People often speak of love as magical, fated, or sacred. Yet love did not begin in poetry or religion. It began in biology.

Love is not a supernatural gift. It is an evolved emotional instinct — a built-in part of the human mind.

This does not make love smaller. It explains why love is so large in our lives.

Love exists because it helped our ancestors survive, raise vulnerable children, build families, and create bonds strong enough to weather hardship. Nature selected for beings who could feel love deeply, fiercely, and sometimes painfully — because those who did left more descendants than those who did not.

To understand love’s origins is not to steal its mystery. It is to see the mystery clearly.

---

## What Instinct Means

Scientists use the word *instinct* in two ways, and both matter.

### 1. Instinct as Fixed Behavior

In many animals, instinct means **complex unlearned actions that unfold automatically** once triggered. Examples include:

- spiders spinning characteristic webs  
- stickleback fish attacking anything with a red underside  
- sea turtles crawling to the ocean after hatching  
- newborn kangaroos crawling into the pouch  
- honeybees performing the waggle dance to guide hive-mates to food  

These are sometimes called **fixed action patterns**. They are built-in sequences.

Love is not this type of instinct.

### 2. Instinct as Emotional Motivation

Humans also have instincts that work differently: **emotional systems that push us gently but powerfully toward certain behaviors.**

Fear encourages caution.  
Hunger drives eating.  
Parental feelings drive care.  

And love — romantic and parental — steers us toward bonding, loyalty, and devotion.

> Love is an evolved emotional tendency that guides us toward connection and caregiving because those who formed strong bonds were more likely to survive and raise children.

Love is not automatic. It is not mechanical. But it is deeply built into us.

## Why Love Exists

Love evolved because it worked.

### Attraction

Brings people together.

### Infatuation

Focuses attention on a partner, narrowing the world.

### Attachment

Holds relationships together long enough to raise children.

### Parental Love

Makes protecting and nurturing offspring feel essential, not optional.

If love felt casual, it would fail. It must feel profound to do its job.

---

## The Brain Tools Behind Love

Love uses ancient brain systems:

| System | Role |
|---|---|
| Dopamine | Motivation, focus, longing |
| Oxytocin & Vasopressin | Bonding, trust, loyalty |
| Endorphins | Comfort, safety, emotional warmth |

Chemicals do not “explain away” love. They *enable* it — the way nerves enable touch or eyes enable sight.

## Responding to Other Views Respectfully

### Religion

Many believe love reflects the divine. A natural explanation does not deny anyone's faith. Science describes the *mechanism*; faith addresses *meaning*.

### Idealism

Some say love is too deep to be biological. But depth does not disappear when we understand roots. Vision is biological — beauty remains real.

### Behaviorism

Old theories said love was only learned behavior. We now know infants bond before learning, and so do many animals.

### Social Construction

Culture shapes love’s expression — weddings, dating customs, songs — but it does not create the warm rush in a parent holding a newborn, or the ache of missing someone beloved.

Culture writes verses; biology gave us the paper and ink.

---

## What Understanding Adds

Knowing that love evolved does not deflate it. It makes love more personal, not less.

When we love someone, we are part of a story that stretches back millions of years, through countless parents, mates, and children who survived because their hearts could not help but care.

Love is not an illusion. It is one of nature’s greatest inventions.

## A Lyrical Closing

We do not love because angels whisper to us. We love because ancestors held each other through wind and fire, because children once clung to parents under cold stars, because two early humans walked through danger side by side and the ones who stayed together lived to see another dawn.

Love is written in our bones and blood. And yet — we are the only creature who can pause and notice it, name it, sing to it, promise by it, break for it, and build poems and cities around it.

Understanding does not drain the magic. It reveals where the magic came from.

Love is instinct. Love is nature. Love is real — not beyond biology, but born from it.

And that truth is not smaller than myth. It is larger than myth, because it belongs to the world we truly inhabit and to every beating heart that ever dared to care.
